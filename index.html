BIMD in The Design School

BIMD stands for:BA (Hons) in Interactive Multimedia in Design. It is a three year degree programme designed to provide specialist education for students intending to enter the multimedia/new media design industries within the context of multi-platform delivery as Information Architects, Design/Project Managers, Web Designers Interaction Designers, Games Designers and Experiantial Designers. We collaborate with Northumbria University UK. We have a Unique partnership that helps our students get DUAL AWARDS

FACILITIES

Design Studio

The Design Studio is a place for students and design teachers to come togethr in a teaching-learning context. Iit is a splace where students carry out the practical application of design, producing and refining design ideas ans assembling these into tangible forms for presentation and critiques. It is in the studios that the students develop their understanding and working philosophies of design.

Design Mac Lab

The design Mac Lab is essential to design study. Designers have become reliant on digital technology and it would be impossible for a graduate practicioner to secure ajob without knowledge of the latest industry standard hardware and software. Computer Labs are equipped with the necessary peripheral equpment such as printers, scanners and access to the internet.

Photo Studio

Photography is an essential aspect of design practice. A photographic studio where students learn the use of artifical lighting to photograph a variety of objects will strengthen their understanding of the visual communication process. It is equipped with a range of lamps, diffusers, reflectors, and a variety of different backdrop materials. Computers linked to the cameras allow students to see and manipulate the images captured.

Sonic Labs

Sound plays an imprtnat role in the school of multimedia. It is used in vidoes, animations and games. Therefore Tylors provides their students a fully sound proof room, where they can record their desired sounds with no external/outdoor sounds. The room is equipped with mics, Headphones, sound mixers, computers. the students have the acess to eidt nd change their sounds thorugh the computer lab specially designed for ausio editing.

CODA Gallery

Centre of Design and Architecture (CODA). A space where student works are displayed is essential to the development of students. This allows the sharing of ideas and junior students are provided with an opportunity to view the standard of work by seniors, which will be expected of them as they progress trhough the course. As their works display in a public setting, students are encouraged to reflect on items produced and observe the response of viewers to these works. This provides invalubale feedback to students as design products are designed with a mass audience in muind. Observing the different responses allow to understand the users needs.

A Day at the Arcade

The IMD semester 3 students were taken at the Arcade of Sunway Pyramid. Ech student was given 3 game tokens. The purpose was for the students to see the different types of game interface for their module Game Design. They were taken by their lecturer Mr Razif Mohammed.

Martin Junior tops Hyundai Rev-It-Up Challenge Take 2

A classy video that showcases the beauty of the brand new Hyundai Veloster won Martin Junior, a group of students from The Design School (TDS) at Taylor’s University the champion title at the Hyundai Rev-It-Up Challenge Take 2 TV Commercial Competition. Competing against two other teams who made the cut to the final, the commercial video produced by Martin Junior, under the guidance of their lecturer, Mr. Martin Chong, was a clear winner with its short but compact interpretation of the new car.

Conquering the IDCS Spade Awards

Students from The Design School at Taylor’s University win big at the international competition for two consecutive years

Students of The Design School at Taylor’s University (TDS) show their true capabilities and talents as they grabbed an impressive total of seven awards at the recent Interior Design Confederation Singapore (IDCS) SPADE Awards, en route to helping Taylor’s University clinched the prestigious ‘Institution of SPADE Awards 2012’. Competing with over 50 finalists from Singapore and Australia, the six students from Taylor’s University who made it to the final have all returned with prizes in hands.
