BA (HONS) Interactive Multimedia Design

BIMD stands for: BA (Hons) in Interactive Multimedia in Design. It is a three year degree programme designed to provide specialist education for students intending to enter the multimedia/new media design industries within the context of multi-platform delivery as Information Architects, Design/Project Managers, Web Designers Interaction Designers, Games Designers and Experiantial Designers.

Modules will combine core web and interaction design skills with an intellectual understanding of information design, design/project managements, enterprise, and innovation cutlures to produce well- rounded and enterprise oriented students ready to meet the challanges of working withing Interactive Multimedia Design.

WHAT YOU WILL LEARN

  • Visual communication principles, creative thinking/problem solving approaches, understanding of contemporary and historical design issues, presentation skills, through a combination of studio-based project work, laboratory-based learning, lectures and tutorials, site visits and internships
  • Design Increasingly complex interactive multimedia work in response to set problems with understanding/consideration of the demands of the global industry.
  • Knowledge and skills to use a variety of media to communicate design solutions, ranging from simple paper based material to highly sophisticated digital software and hardware.

A t the end of Year 1 and 2, students will undergo 7 weeks Practicum (internship) with a design company or advertising agency. During their internship, students will be introduced to the real environment of the crative industries and would gain a broader design experience and knowledge. Students will present their observations in the form of a log book and a report, linking their experiences with the theoratical understanding developed during the course. This is an opportunity for the students to enhance and strengthen their characters and portfolio.

ASSESSMENTS

There is a mixture of coursework and examinations in this programme, depending on the nature of the subject and its objectives. For design subjects, assessment is mainly formative i.e. students are assessed as they work and learn. Every practical design assignment is marked and graded and students will receive feedback on how to improve their performance within a very short time. Assignment marks will contribute towards an overall mark/grade at the end of the semester. Theory - related subjects require students to write essays and produce reports, sometimes in visual form. There may be written examinations for such subjects.
